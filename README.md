**The story behind this:**
Florida heat is no joke. One week of extreme temperatures or an unexpected frost can wipe out months of careful gardening. As a homemaker who grows plants and relies on them, I needed a real solution — not just a weather app, but a system that monitors MY soil, MY garden, MY conditions in real time and warns me before it is too late.

**What I am building:**

### 🥈 Priority 2A — Building NOW (alongside Plant Monitor)
#### 🛒 Homeland Market

**The story behind this:**
When you leave your homeland, you leave behind more than a place. You leave behind the taste of your mother's cooking — the specific vegetables, the herbs, the traditional medicines, the flavors that no American grocery store carries. For thousands of Burmese, Southeast Asian, and immigrant families living in the US, finding homeland ingredients means driving hours, paying expensive import prices, or simply going without.

I am building this because I know that feeling personally. And I know thousands of families — Burmese, Vietnamese, Filipino, Karen, Ethiopian, and more — who feel it every single week when they stand in an American grocery store and cannot find a single ingredient that tastes like home.

Homeland Market connects immigrant and diaspora communities directly with verified suppliers of authentic homeland groceries, fresh produce, traditional herbs, specialty foods, and cultural medicines — with transparent pricing, community reviews, and a bilingual interface that does not leave first-generation immigrants behind.

**What it solves:**
- Immigrant families cannot find authentic homeland ingredients in local stores
- Small ethnic grocery suppliers have no affordable platform to reach their own community
- Language barriers make mainstream online grocery platforms inaccessible
- No existing marketplace serves multilingual immigrant communities with cultural understanding

**What I am building:**
- SQL database of verified suppliers, products, and inventory by cultural category
- HTML/CSS storefront — clean, bilingual (English + Burmese), mobile-friendly
- Search and filter by cultural community, product type, and region of origin
- Community review system in multiple languages — so families trust what they buy
- Future expansion: serve Vietnamese, Filipino, Karen, Rohingya, and all immigrant communities

**Dedicated to:** Every immigrant family who misses the taste of home — and every small supplier trying to serve their community.

`SQL` `HTML/CSS` `Python` `Database Design` `Bilingual UI` `Community Platform`

### 🏅 Priority 2C — Building after SQL Advanced complete
#### 🔍 Community Price-Drop Radar *(Retail Rollback Aggregator)*

**The story behind this:**
Inflation is not a statistic. It is the moment you open your grocery bill and realize that the same everyday essentials that fit comfortably in your family's budget two years ago now stretch it to the breaking point — every single week.

The numbers are not opinions. They are government-documented facts:

According to the **USDA Economic Research Service**, vegetable oil prices rose more than **50% in 2021 alone** and **doubled compared to 2020 levels** by 2022. The **US Bureau of Labor Statistics** confirms that fats and oils — basic cooking staples in every American household — saw a historically large price increase of **18 to 19 percent in 2022 alone**. Cereals and bakery products rose **16.1%**. Dairy products rose **15.3%**. These are not luxury items. These are the groceries that families buy every week to survive.

I feel this personally. My community feels this. Every low-income and middle-income family in this country feels this at the checkout line.

But buried inside the same retail system charging these inflated prices are rollback events, clearance sales, and extreme temporary price drops that most families never find — because they are scattered, unpredictable, and invisible unless you know exactly where and when to look.

This project is built to find those opportunities automatically and put them in front of the families who need them most. When an essential item drops significantly below its normal price, that is real money staying inside a family's budget. Multiplied across thousands of families and hundreds of rollback events per week, this tool has the potential to return meaningful savings to the communities that need it most.

**What it solves:**
- Families hit by documented government-confirmed inflation cannot easily find rollback deals and extreme discounts
- Deep clearance events are temporary, scattered, and invisible to most shoppers
- No free, community-focused tool centralizes high-value price drops for essential household goods
- Low-income and immigrant families — already hit hardest by inflation — have the least time and resources to hunt for deals

**What I am building:**
- Python scripts pulling real-time price and discount data from publicly available retailer APIs
- Filters isolating only extreme discounts — ignoring standard pricing noise, surfacing only high-yield savings
- SQL database logging historical price changes over time — so the tool learns what is a genuine rollback vs a fake "sale"
- Simple, accessible interface showing today's best deals on essentials: groceries, household goods, medicine
- Future: alert system — notify families when a tracked essential item hits their target price threshold
- Future: bilingual interface (English + Burmese) so immigrant families can access savings in their language

**Data sources — all government and public:**
- US Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) — Consumer Price Index, average food prices
- USDA Economic Research Service — food price inflation data and commodity tracking
- Federal Reserve FRED database — historical price index data
- Publicly available retailer API data where permitted under their terms of service

**Why this matters beyond saving money:**
According to the USDA Economic Research Service, fats and oils showed a historically large price increase of 18 to 19 percent for 2022, and vegetable oil costs doubled since 2020. These are not isolated products — they represent the foundation of every family's grocery basket. When essential prices double, the families with the least financial cushion suffer the most. This tool is for them.

**Dedicated to:** Every family doing the checkout-line math. Every parent choosing between two essential items because they cannot afford both. Every community member who deserves real savings — not just coupons for things they do not need.

`Python` `SQL` `BLS Data` `USDA Data` `Public APIs` `Data Pipelines` `Community Analytics` `Price Intelligence`
